I am a woman. Hear me roar.

A woman with a voice is, by definition, a strong woman.

Women have been deprived of their rights from the beginning of time. Despite the efforts made in the early 20th century by the suffragettes and the recognition of women’s rights in nearly the entire world, women are still in the disadvantageous position in modern societies.

That, however, did not stop them from chasing their dreams and finally realizing them. It may have been more difficult for them, but nothing ever proved to be impossible for them to achieve.

All women are, therefore, strong women. All women have a voice to raise. All women roar. They just need someone to listen to them.

That is what our organization and dozens of others are aiming for.

Women’s rights are human rights! Dignity, respect, and justice for all.

No woman should be stripped from her rights just because of her sex.

We are all entitled to human rights. These include the right to live free from violence and discrimination; to enjoy the highest attainable standard of physical and mental health; to be educated; to own property; to vote; and to earn an equal wage.

But across the globe many women and girls still face discrimination on the basis of sex and gender. Gender inequality underpins many problems which disproportionately affect women and girls, such as domestic and sexual violence, lower pay, lack of access to education, and inadequate healthcare. For many years women’s rights movements have fought hard to address this inequality, campaigning to change laws or taking to the streets to demand their rights are respected.

There is no limit as to what we, women, can accomplish.

The question isn’t who’s going to let me; it’s who is going to stop me.

It is true that women are at a disadvantage because of their gender. Many attribute this inequality to the fact that "it is in the nature of a woman" not to be able to take on the same responsibilities as those of the opposite sex. This perception, however, could not be further from reality.

Women have repeatedly proven that sky is their only limit. From Nobel Prizes (Marie Curie: Nobel Prize in Physics 1903 and Nobel Prize in Chemistry 1911 and Malala Yousafzai: Nobel Peace Prize 2014) to high-ranking positions in government (Kamala Harris: Vice-President of the United States), women have denied the stereotypes that define them in society countless times.

We said it once and we will say it again: There is not a single restriction as to what we, WOMEN, can accomplish.
